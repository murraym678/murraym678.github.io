TASK : Get familiar with the normal expression patterns for EVEN-SKIPPED.

The development of an embryo is a fluid, continuous process. Here are a couple of time-lapse movies from youtube to help you visualise the dynamics involved.

 

Below you see the changing EVE expression pattern from stage 5 through to stage 13. As you saw above one stage smoothly follows onto the next so the EVE pattern also changes smoothly. For example, the transition from stages 5-9 and into stage 10 sees the loss of the epidermal stripes and the onset of expression in the pericardial cells, with the CNS neuronal cells coming on during stage 11. So when you are looking at your embryo slides keep in mind that you may encounter embryos at transitional stages.

Stage 5 At stage 5, the 7 pair-rule stripes of Even-Skipped expression become clear and evenly spaced.

Stage 8-9 Shortly afterwards the embryo begins to elongate which means that the EVE stripes become spaced further apart. Since the embryo is constrained within in the egg-shell this elongation requires the embryo to wrap-around, onto the dorsal side

Stage 10 Germband elongation stops by stage 10. By this time the stripes have faded and a new population of cells becomes visible as a ring around the tip of the germ band. The pericardial cells also become visible during this stage.

Stage 11 During stage 11, segmental bulges and furrows become apparent and EVE expression is activated in a subset of neural and mesodermal cells

Stage 12 At stage 12 the germ-band retracts again and the EVE positive clusters become clearer.

Stage 13 Following retraction, the epidermis on either side of the embryo extends dorsally, eventually meeting at the dorsal midline, enclosing the amnioserosa (as) (a tissue that does not contribute to the final animal).
